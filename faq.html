Data Columns Explained

What does each column mean?
Symbol: The ticker symbol of the ETF.
Name: The full name of the ETF.
Fund Provider: The company that manages the ETF.
Dividend Yield: The current annual dividend yield.
Expense Ratio: The annual fee charged by the fund as a percentage of assets.
AUM: Assets Under Management – shows the size and liquidity of the fund.
Total Return: Overall performance including dividends and price return.
Price Decay: Measures how the ETF price has performed relative to its inception.
Inception Date: When the ETF was launched.
Payout Frequency: How often dividends are paid (weekly, monthly, quarterly, etc.).
Rating: Our overall opinion based on yield, performance, price decay, underlying holdings, and fund provider reputation.
What is the Tax Treatment Grade?
The Tax Treatment Grade (A–F) reflects how tax-friendly we believe the ETF is for a typical investor holding it in a taxable brokerage account. Grade A is the most tax-efficient, while Grade F is the least tax-friendly.

Important: Everyone’s tax situation is different. This grade is based on our opinion and general observations only. It is not tax advice. We are not tax professionals. Please consult with a qualified tax advisor or accountant for advice specific to your situation.
What does the Rating mean?
Our Rating is an overall score (A–F) based on multiple factors including:
  • Dividend Yield
  • Total Return performance
  • Price Decay
  • Underlying holdings quality
  • Fund provider reputation and track record
  • Expense ratio and overall efficiency
It is our opinion only and should be used as one data point in your research, not as a buy or sell recommendation.
